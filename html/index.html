I am Xiaoyu XU, an Assistant Professor in the Department of English at City University of Hong Kong. I hold a Ph.D. in Applied Linguistics (UK), an MA TESOL (UK), a BA in Sociology (China) and a BSc in Accounting and Finance (China). I have previously worked as an English teacher at a secondary school in China as well as a research assistant and lecturer for the Department of English and Languages at Coventry University. My research interests include English for Academic Purposes, English for Specific Purposes, English-medium Instruction, TESOL, Sociolinguistics, Corpus Linguistics, Discourse Analysis, and Intercultural Communication. I have published in prestigious international journals such as Journal of English for Academic Purposes, English for Specific Purposes and Text & Talk.

My academic career has been strongly influenced by my Ph.D. supervisors, Prof. Hilary Nesi and Prof. Sheena Gardner, two leading experts in EAP teaching and learning and corpora linguistics. My first project (Ph.D.) investigated stance and voice in academic discourse across cultures. It was highly praised by the viva panel which included Prof. Susan Hunston as the external examiner. The second project I took part in explored the language of engineering English from the perspective of SFL register, under the supervision of Prof. Sheena Gardner.

In 2018, I moved to Hong Kong and since then have been working as an assistant professor at City University of Hong Kong. I am currently working on two funded projects on academic discourse as the principal investigator 1) How Do Novice Academics and Experienced Academics Interact with Audience in Computer Science Conference Discussion Sessions? 2) and A Corpus-based Analysis of Engagement Language in Successful Engineering MOOC Lectures.
